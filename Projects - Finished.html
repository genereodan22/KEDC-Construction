San Rafael Eco Oil Gas Station

The San Rafael ECO Oil Gas Station located at San Rafael Bulacan is project by KEDC is a groundbreaking initiative that aims to revolutionize the fuel industry.

The project involves the construction of an eco-friendly gas station that utilizes renewable energy sources and sustainable practices.

The San Rafael ECO Oil Gas Station Project is a shining example of what can be achieved when we prioritize sustainability and innovation. As more companies and governments follow in the footsteps of KEDC, we can look forward to a brighter, more sustainable future for all.


San Francisco ECO Oil Gas Station

San Francisco ECO Oil is located at San Francisco San Jose Del Monte Bulacan the design and construction of the San Francisco ECO Oil Gas Station is being led by a team of experienced architects, engineers, and construction professionals. The team is using the latest sustainable building practices and materials to ensure that the gas station is energy-efficient and environmentally friendly.

The gas station will feature a unique design that incorporates natural light and ventilation to reduce energy consumption. The construction process will also be closely monitored to ensure that waste is minimized and materials are recycled whenever possible.

The MUZON Eco Oil Gas Station Project are numerous and far-reaching.

Emilio Jacinto Isolation Project

The Emilio Jacinto Isolation Facility Project by KEDC is a state-of-the-art medical facility designed to combat infectious diseases and pandemics. The project was initiated in response to the COVID-19 outbreak, with the aim of providing a safe and secure environment for patients to receive treatment and recover.

The facility is equipped with the latest medical technology and staffed by highly trained healthcare professionals who are dedicated to providing the best possible care to patients. The Emilio Jacinto Isolation Facility Project is a testament to KEDC's commitment to public health and safety.


EDSA Eco Oil Gas Station

The ECO Oil Gas Station offers numerous benefits to both the environment and consumers. The incorporation of electric vehicle charging stations promotes the use of clean energy sources, reducing harmful emissions.

The ECO Oil Gas Station with Charging Station Project by KEDC is an ambitious project that aims to provide sustainable energy solutions to our communities.

Through the incorporation of electric vehicle charging stations, green infrastructure, and state-of-the-art equipment, the project sets a new standard for the gas station industry. The project's impact on the environment, economy, and society makes it a valuable addition to the region.


SEAMEO Innotech Roofing

SEAMEO Innotech Roofing is a project of the Kennedy Energy Development Corp. (KEDC) that aims to provide sustainable roofing solutions for schools and communities. The project focuses on using innovative materials and techniques to create durable and cost-effective roofs that can withstand the region's harsh weather conditions.

SEAMEO Innotech Roofing was established in response to the growing need for safe and reliable infrastructures. With climate change causing more frequent natural disasters, it is essential to have roofs that can protect students and teachers from the elements.


DPWH San Bartolome Roadnetwork and Pipelaying Project

The San Bartolome Roadnetwork and Pipelaying Project by KEDC is a major infrastructure development initiative aimed at enhancing transportation and water supply in the region.

The project involves the construction of a network of roads and pipelines that will connect various parts of the San Bartolome area, providing residents with better access to essential services and amenities.


Cubao Elementary School Rectification Project

Cubao Elementary School Rectification Project is a government initiative aimed at improving the quality of education in one of the most populated areas of Quezon City, Philippines. The project involves the renovation and expansion of the school's infrastructure to accommodate the growing number of students and provide them with a better learning environment.

The rectification project was launched in response to the deteriorating condition of the school's facilities, which posed a serious threat to the safety and well-being of the students. The project aims to address these issues and create a more conducive learning environment for the students.


Payatas Elementary School Rehabilitation Project

Payatas Elementary School is a public school located in Quezon City, Philippines. It serves students from kindergarten to grade six and has been in operation for over 50 years. Unfortunately, the school's facilities have deteriorated over time, making it difficult for students to learn in a safe and conducive environment.

In response, the KEDC (Kennedy Energy and Development Corp.) launched a rehabilitation project to improve the school's infrastructure and provide better learning opportunities for its students.
